Rosehill College Courses & Programmes:
A Complete Guide for International Families
From Year 9 foundations to NCEA Level 3 — including Performing Arts, Kapa Haka, and our dedicated international student support services.
Rosehill College May 2026 8 min read Papakura, Auckland

If you're researching New Zealand secondary schools for your child, one of the first questions on your list is probably: what does the school actually teach, and how does the qualification system work?

This guide covers the full curriculum at Rosehill College — a co-educational public secondary school in Papakura, South Auckland, serving students from Year 9 to Year 13.

How New Zealand Secondary Education Works: The NCEA Framework

New Zealand's national qualification system — NCEA (National Certificate of Educational Achievement) — is credit-based, not exam-based. Students accumulate credits throughout the year through internal assessments and external exams. There is no single high-stakes national exam.

International Recognition
NCEA Level 3 is accepted by universities in New Zealand, Australia, the United Kingdom, Canada, and the United States — giving students a genuinely global qualification pathway from a South Auckland public school.

Rosehill's Signature Programmes

Performing Arts

Rosehill College has one of the strongest Performing Arts programmes among public secondary schools in South Auckland. The school's dedicated Drama and Music facilities were opened in 2000 by Prime Minister Helen Clark — a lasting signal of the school's commitment to arts education.

  • Drama — from Year 9 improvisation through to full theatrical productions at NCEA Level 3
  • Music — classical and contemporary, instrumental and vocal
  • Dance — contemporary, Pacific, and movement-based performance
  • Visual Arts — painting, photography, mixed media, NCEA portfolios

Kapa Haka

🌿 What is Kapa Haka?
Kapa Haka is Māori performing arts — song (waiata), dance, and cultural expression rooted in the indigenous heritage of Aotearoa New Zealand. At Rosehill, it is a living expression of the school's bicultural identity. The school's competitive group has performed at Polyfest, one of New Zealand's largest secondary school cultural festivals. For international students, Kapa Haka offers a direct way to engage with Māori culture and build relationships with New Zealand-born peers.

Sports

Rosehill operates a modern gymnasium and multi-purpose courts, fielding competitive teams across multiple sports. The school's House system — six Houses including Atawhai, Kahurangi, and Pounamu — builds community through inter-house competitions from the first week.

International Student Support

  • English Language Support (ELS) — classes running in parallel with regular subjects for non-native speakers
  • International Student Office — dedicated staff from enrolment through graduation
  • Homestay placement — school-vetted families, preferences accommodated before placement
  • Pastoral care — academic monitoring, peer tutoring, and counselling on campus
  • Fully compliant with New Zealand's Education (Pastoral Care of Tertiary and International Learners) Code 2021
