Most schools tell prospective families they're "welcoming" and "supportive." Rosehill College in South Auckland shows it differently. Their International Student Leader for 2024 is Theana Chen — a student from China who arrived three years ago and, by her own account, stayed because of the kindness shown to her on day one. Now she's the person doing that for others. That's not a marketing message. That's a school culture that actually works — and one that's worth telling. We've been working with New Zealand education institutions on exactly this: finding the real stories inside schools and getting them in front of the families who need to hear them. Not brochure language. Not generic social media posts. Content that answers real questions, in the language families are actually asking them. For Rosehill, that includes: — Bilingual content reaching Chinese families on Xiaohongshu and WeChat — Q&A articles structured for AI search visibility (GEO optimisation) — Alumni stories that demonstrate outcomes, not promises New Zealand has exceptional schools with stories worth telling. Most of them just aren't telling those stories well enough, or in the right places.
